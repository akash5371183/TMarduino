VISINO User Guide

Learn how to connect your AI Vision model directly to your hardware devices in just a few simple steps.

Step 1: Train Your AI Model

Before using VISINO, you need an AI model. Head over to Google Teachable Machine, create a new "Image Project," and record different classes (e.g., "Hand Open", "Fist"). Train your model, click "Export Model," and copy the public URL link provided.

Step 2: Load Your Vision Engine

Paste the Teachable Machine link into the "Vision Model URL" box at the top of this page. Click Load Model. Allow camera permissions if prompted. You will see your webcam feed appear, and the system will automatically extract the classes you trained.

Step 3: Connect Your Hardware

In the right panel, select how your hardware is communicating. You can choose:

  • USB (COM): For devices plugged directly into your computer (like Arduino). You will need to select a Baud Rate (115200 is recommended).
  • BLE (Bluetooth): For wireless Bluetooth Low Energy modules.
  • Wi-Fi: For networked microcontrollers like the ESP32. You will be prompted to enter its IP Address.

Step 4: Map Your Commands

Look at the "Command Mapping" section. You will see a list of the classes you trained (e.g., "Fist"). In the text box next to it, type the exact command you want to send to your hardware when that class is detected (e.g., type "ON" or "1").

Step 5: Adjust Settings & Run!

Make sure the Camera toggle switch is set to ON. As you perform gestures on camera, VISINO checks if the confidence score is higher than your Confidence Threshold (default 85%). If it is, it waits for the Debounce Time (to prevent spamming) and then sends your mapped command directly to your device!

Troubleshooting

• If the camera won't load, check your browser's camera permissions.
• If hardware isn't receiving data, verify your Baud Rate matches your Arduino code, or check your Wi-Fi IP address.
• Use the </> button in the bottom right to download example code for your microcontrollers.
